Wayanad Jilla

The name Wayanad is derived from Vayal Nadu which means the land of paddy fields. It is a picturesque plateau situated at a height between 700 meters and 2100 meters above the mean sea level nested among the mountains of the Western Ghats on the Eastern portion of North Kerala and on the sides of Tamil Nadu and Karnataka States. The District was carved out from the then Kozhikode and Kannur Districts.

sweet spots



If you don’t mind walking a bit, walk up to this pre-historic rock shelter of Edakkal-caves. Edakkal means “stone in between”, so here you have three huge boulders where one rests in between, while one of them serves as a roof. The rock engravings are a major highlight of this place.


Pookot Lake in Wayanad is one of the most beautiful lakes in Kerala surrounded and enveloped by rich, evergreen forests. If you are lucky, you can see groups of blue water lily flowers floating here and there. Boating is allowed here
